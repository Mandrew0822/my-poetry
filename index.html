En Passant

In passing, two soldiers, merely puppets in the fray, Hallowed husks, who don't own their cold, decaying breath, They are only marionettes who live solely for the entity they call master, There exists no soul, no thoughts, and no will not ordained by the grandmaster, Through mist and mud they're made to obey, Each step prescribed like all the rest. Their armor gleams but hearts are carved from alabaster, Two kingdoms wage their games with lives cast faster, No glory found where shadows lay, Like rusted horns bereft of breath, Each swings their blade, as strings command them faster.

The Divine ποιητής

Stars ignite the velvet night, Mountains soar to tear the sky, Oceans roar, and yet are confined, In nature's art, the heart's reply. Sunrise spills in a blossoming rose, Sunset's blaze, like molten gold, Every leaf and creature flows, In the tapestry of life, his love is told. Seasons waltz in cosmic dance, Morning mist and twilight's veil, Hints of a divine romance, In every moment, a second chance. In each breath, a world unfurled, With each detail pearled, Whispers of a love so vast, Echoes of eternity, forever cast.

The Deceased Violinist

A lifeless corpse holds the lively contrivance. The instrument overflows with soothing power. A soothing power that doesn't reach its master. The strings that reverberates with love and kindness It's simply used to mask the deceit. An audience which just hears sweet tones, forgets to ignore the hypocrisy.

Cor Mundatur

In a realm where shadow's bind A wind parts and subdues one's cries The chains which confine one's heart begin to whine The former reign succumbs to the bane A single blossom is birthed from the pain The reformed heart delights in this new light And lives on from this respite
